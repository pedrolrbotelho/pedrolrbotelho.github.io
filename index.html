  • Short Bio

  • I am a visiting student in the Communications and Networking Laboratory at Northwestern University, working with Prof. Igor Kadota. I received my Bachelor of Science in Electronic Engineering from Instituto Tecnológico de Aeronáutica - ITA, Brazil’s top-ranked engineering school (acceptance rate of 0.98%).

    My research interest lies in the intersection of reinforcement learning, causal inference, and multi-agent systems. I'm interested in how to build trusted and generalizable agents that can successfully cooperate.

    Consider a physics student who lacks a firm grasp of the theory underlying Maxwell’s equations and what they express mathematically. If the student prepared for an electromagnetism exam exclusively by studying numerous solved problems from past exams, they are likely to perform poorly when confronted with questions that differ substantially from those previously studied. Modern artificial intelligence systems often exhibit a similar limitation: strong performance on familiar data but poor generalization to new scenarios.

    I believe that Causal Decision-Making and Causal Reinforcement Learning can address these limitations by combining data and structural invariances about the environment to reason about questions of counterfactual nature and dealing with confounded observations.

    Feel free to contact me if you are interested in working on (multi-agent/causal) reinforcement learning!
